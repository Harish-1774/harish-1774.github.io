Pick and Place using Imitation Learning

A single-box pick-and-place policy for a Franka Panda in Isaac Lab. The student is an action-chunking transformer with a CVAE-style latent (ACT-CVAE): it reads a short history of low-dimensional state — end-effector position and yaw, not a quaternion — and predicts a chunk of EE-delta actions. Training starts from an IK expert, then continues with state-based DAgger to correct covariate shift.

Task

The scene has one coloured cube and one matching bin on a table, randomized within the reachable workspace. A Franka Panda must grasp the cube and place it in the bin. Success is when the cube settles inside the bin within spatial tolerances.

The policy is state-based: it sees proprioception, end-effector pose (xyz + yaw), and object/bin poses — not camera images. That keeps the model compact and focuses the experiment on action chunking and CVAE behaviour rather than visual representation learning.

End-to-End Pipeline

  1. Collect privileged IK expert demos at 60 Hz (100 episodes, 16 parallel envs, horizon 2000).
  2. Downsample ×2 to 30 Hz so training sequences cover more wall-clock motion without lengthening the observation window.
  3. Train ACT-CVAE with behavioural cloning (z-score normalization, cartesian distances scaled ×100).
  4. Optional DAgger warm-started from the BC checkpoint.
  5. Evaluate with closed-loop rollouts.

Expert Demonstrations

Labels come from a privileged differential-IK pick-and-place solver — a finite-state machine, not a learned policy: approach → descend → grasp (with dwell) → lift → approach place → descend → release → lift after. Grasp yaw is aligned to the cube yaw. End-effector speed defaults to 0.75 m/s.

Demos are stored as scaled EE deltas: (target − current) × 10 for xyz and yaw, plus a binary gripper command. Scaling keeps small metric deltas in a numerically friendly range for regression.

Observation space

  • Agent (13D): proprioception 9D (joint pos/vel) + EE xyz 3D + EE yaw 1D.
  • Objects (7D): cube xyz (EE frame) + cube yaw (robot frame) + bin xyz (EE frame).
  • Total state ≈ 20D per timestep — no cameras, no quaternions.

Action space

The policy outputs 5D: Δxyz + Δyaw + gripper. At deployment an IK converter maps EE deltas to the environment's relative joint-position arm action plus gripper.

ACT-CVAE Network

The policy is an Action Chunking Transformer with a CVAE-style encoder — following the ACT idea of predicting a short future action sequence rather than a single step, which reduces compounding error.

[proprio ∥ objects] × T=16
  → Linear–ReLU–Linear → width 128
  → Transformer encoder (2 layers, 4 heads)
  → Transformer decoder (2 layers, chunk queries)
  → Linear–ReLU–Linear → (chunk size 4, action dim 5)

CVAE: action chunk → style encoder (2 layers) → z ∈ R³²
Training: L = MSE(actions) + 10 · KL(q(z|a) ∥ N(0,I))
Inference: z = 0 (prior mean) → deterministic policy

Encoder

  • Concatenate proprio and object state each step, project to a 128-dimensional embedding.
  • Add learned positional embeddings over an observation window of 16 timesteps — temporal context without an LSTM hidden state.
  • Run a 2-layer Transformer encoder (4 heads, FFN 256, GELU, dropout 0.1). Compact width suits ~20D state rather than vision features.

Decoder (action chunking)

  • Learned query embeddings of length 4 cross-attend to encoder memory.
  • A small MLP head maps each query to one action in the 4-step chunk.
  • Predicting a short horizon of future actions lets the policy plan through grasp/release transitions instead of reacting myopically one step at a time.

CVAE style latent

A style encoder (CLS token + projected action tokens, 2 Transformer layers) produces μ and log σ² for a latent z ∈ ℝ³². During training, z is sampled from q(z|a) and concatenated onto encoder memory so the decoder is conditioned on demonstration style. The loss adds a KL term with weight 10 — strong prior regularization in the ACT spirit.

At inference, z is set to 0 (the prior mean). That keeps deployment deterministic: the expert itself is near-deterministic (scripted IK), so a single mode is preferred over sampling multimodal actions online.

Temporal ensembling

At runtime the policy keeps the last 16 observations. With temporal ensembling enabled, it re-predicts a chunk every step and blends overlapping predictions with an exponential weight (coeff 0.01) so newer predictions dominate — smoothing end-effector motion without open-loop chunk execution.

Training Process

Dataset sampling

Training examples are drawn from the full demonstration set. For each end time t, the loader builds an observation window spanning the previous 16 steps (left-padded at episode starts) and an action chunk of the next 4 steps, with pad masks where needed. States and actions are z-score normalized from train-split statistics; cartesian distances are scaled to centimetres (×100).

Sampling from the aggregate of all demos (and later DAgger data) avoids overfitting to a narrow subset of trajectories. Near-pick frames are upweighted (boost ×5 within 0.10 m) so grasp alignment gets more gradient signal.

Behavioural cloning

  • Epochs: 150
  • Batch size: 64
  • Optimizer: Adam, lr = 1×10⁻⁴
  • Loss: MSE on action chunks + 10 × KL (CVAE)
  • Hidden dim / heads: 128 / 4
  • Encoder / decoder layers: 2 / 2
  • Seq length / chunk size: 16 / 4

On a completed single-box ACT-CVAE run, train loss fell from roughly 0.70 to 0.008 over 150 epochs. Offline validation reached EE-position MSE ≈ 0.005, yaw MSE ≈ 0.0003, and gripper-sign agreement ≈ 99.9%. These are open-loop fit metrics; closed-loop success is addressed by the DAgger stage below.

State DAgger

After BC, the same ACT-CVAE weights are refined with state DAgger: β-mixed rollouts, expert labels on every visited state, append to a growing aggregate dataset, then a fixed number of gradient steps on the full set. Defaults: 5 iterations × 20 episodes × 2000 grad steps, β starting at 1.0, 10 parallel envs. Sampling from the aggregate keeps earlier expert coverage in the mix and reduces bias toward the latest on-policy batch.

Why These Design Choices

  • Single box + pos/yaw (no quat) — one cube and bin with EE yaw instead of a quaternion keeps the observation compact (~20D) while still exposing grasp orientation.
  • Action chunks (4) — classic ACT fix for compounding error on temporally extended manipulation.
  • Obs window (16) — explicit history without LSTM state; enough context for approach and grasp timing.
  • Compact transformer (128, 2+2) — matched to low-dim state, fast enough for closed-loop Isaac Lab rollouts.
  • CVAE with z = 0 at test — capacity for multimodal demos during training, deterministic control at deploy because the expert itself is deterministic.
  • EE-delta labels + IK — policy reasons in Cartesian space; joints are recovered by a fixed converter.
  • 60 Hz → 30 Hz downsample — longer physical horizon per sequence without growing the observation window.
